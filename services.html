EcoRudder

In the EcoRudder project, I played a pivotal role in the development of sustainable rudders for Airbus airplanes. Collaborating with a team of experts, we utilized advanced materials like PPS, PEI, PEEK, and Nomex honeycomb unit cell to create homogenized material models and geometric FE models. Through meticulous parametric studies, we analyzed diverse load cases, focusing on crafting thermoplastic rudders for the Airbus A320 that are not only robust but also environmentally friendly and recyclable. Our efforts extended to conceptualizing innovative designs aimed at enhancing both performance and sustainability.

  • Homegenisation
  • Parametric modeling
  • FEA/FEM analysis

Hydrogen Demonstrator and Development Environment (HyDDEn)

My responsibilities revolved around the meticulouscharacterization of CFRP materials intended for fuel tank applications with hydrogen fuel in helicopters/drones. This entailed a comprehensive analysis of the mechanical and leakage behavior of various CFRP variants, such as CF-PEEK, CF-LM-PAEK, and CF-PPS, to evaluate their suitability for fuel tank usage. Leveraging the AFPT (Automated Fiber Placement Technology) process, I facilitated the fabrication of precise test samples to ensure consistent testing conditions. Subsequently, I conducted thorough mechanical testing, microscopy, and permeation testing to assess the structural integrity and performance of these materials across diverse scenarios. Collaborating closely with a multidisciplinary team, I interpreted test results and provided valuable insights for material selection and application.

  • Material characterization
  • AFP Technology
  • Micrographic laminate study

CFD analysis of NASA 37 stage

Utilizing ANSYS CFX and Turbogrid, I spearheaded comprehensive simulations within the aerospace domain. Notably, I modeled the intricate flow dynamics within the stator hub cavity for NASA stage 37 rotor and stator compressor, conducting detailed 3D flow analyses over the compressor blade row. My contributions extended to conducting Mach number, velocity contour, and meridional flow distribution studies, elucidating critical operational parameters and refining compressor maps for enhanced performance.

  • Secondary flows
  • Computational fluid dynamics (CFD)
  • Compressor Map

Experimental investigation of nano fluids for heat rejection

Combining advanced nano fluid technology with experimental skill, I spearheaded a pioneering investigation into heat transfer enhancement within double pipe heat exchangers. Employing MWCNT and Al2O3 nano fluids as working media, I meticulously determined and compared the performance metrics of the heat exchanger. Through rigorous experimentation and theoretical analysis, I precisely evaluated heat transfer coefficients, thermal performance factors, and the effectiveness of the heat exchanger under varying flow conditions. Notably, my contributions revealed significant enhancements in heat transfer coefficients, Reynolds numbers, and Nusselt numbers with increasing mass flow rates. Furthermore, the comparative analysis showcased the superior performance of MWCNT over Al2O3 in augmenting heat transfer efficiency. Ultimately, my efforts culminated in the identification of optimal operational parameters—200 LPH of hot nano fluid and 400 LPH of cold water—yielding exceptional thermal performance factors and effectiveness of 61.59% and 0.507, respectively.

  • NanoFluids
  • Design & Development
  • Thermal & Hydrostatic Analysis
